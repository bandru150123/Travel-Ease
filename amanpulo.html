Set in the idyllic Cuyo Archipelago of Northern Palawan is Amanpulo: a blissful escape with a back-to-nature ethos. The resort is the only establishment on its island of Pamalican, which looks pulled from a postcard (or a daydream) with its lush greenery, white sand beaches and crystalline waters.

Accommodations come in the forms of Casitas and Villas, with 16 of the latter and 42 of the former. All are distributed about the island, lending a welcome feeling of privacy, and each comes with its own golf cart to make getting around the island a breeze. The Casitas are built into the forest canopy, either perched on the hillside or set alongside the beach. Villas up the wow factor a notch with their beach frontages and private pools. Inside, all accommodations are heavenly hideaways set in earth tones with plenty of gorgeous natural wood. High vaulted ceilings make the large spaces feel even roomier, and enormous windows capture spellbinding views. Perks are plentiful, mod cons like Nespresso machines come standard and luxurious bathrooms are huge.

Amanpulo has a good amount of variety as far as dining goes, with something to suit every mood and occasion. On the western side of the island, you’ll find the Beachclub, where you can feast on Mediterranean cuisine with your toes in the sand. Sushi, sashimi and Washoku-inspired cuisines can be found at Nama, the resort's signature Japanese restaurant. Have a casual Italian lunch at the open-air Picnic Grove, or savour Filipino favourites at the Clubhouse Restaurant on an outdoor terrace overlooking the sea.

No island escape is complete without some serious rest and relaxation, and the Aman Spa is an idyllic spot for some pampering. The peaceful venue is set at the island's highest point, and restorative treatments draw upon the traditional healing arts of the Philippines. Nearby, the yoga and meditation pavilion is a beautiful spot for a workout, as is the state-of-the-art fitness centre and tennis courts.

Active guests are extremely well catered to at Amanpulo, with all manner of activities from hiking and scuba diving to trying your hand at a jungle obstacle course. Adrenaline junkies should look into the Extreme Water Sports Retreat; a three-night retreat that takes fitness to the next level with everything from beach runs and kite surfing sessions to open water swims and a hike to the summit of Mount English.

Set in the idyllic Cuyo Archipelago of Northern Palawan is Amanpulo: a blissful escape with a back-to-nature ethos. The resort is the only establishment on its island of Pamalican, which looks pulled from a postcard (or a daydream) with its lush greenery, white sand beaches and crystalline waters.

Accommodations come in the forms of Casitas and Villas, with 16 of the latter and 42 of the former. All are distributed about the island, lending a welcome feeling of privacy, and each comes with its own golf cart to make getting around the island a breeze. The Casitas are built into the forest canopy, either perched on the hillside or set alongside the beach. Villas up the wow factor a notch with their beach frontages and private pools. Inside, all accommodations are heavenly hideaways set in earth tones with plenty of gorgeous natural wood. High vaulted ceilings make the large spaces feel even roomier, and enormous windows capture spellbinding views. Perks are plentiful, mod cons like Nespresso machines come standard and luxurious bathrooms are huge.

Amanpulo has a good amount of variety as far as dining goes, with something to suit every mood and occasion. On the western side of the island, you’ll find the Beachclub, where you can feast on Mediterranean cuisine with your toes in the sand. Sushi, sashimi and Washoku-inspired cuisines can be found at Nama, the resort's signature Japanese restaurant. Have a casual Italian lunch at the open-air Picnic Grove, or savour Filipino favourites at the Clubhouse Restaurant on an outdoor terrace overlooking the sea.

No island escape is complete without some serious rest and relaxation, and the Aman Spa is an idyllic spot for some pampering. The peaceful venue is set at the island's highest point, and restorative treatments draw upon the traditional healing arts of the Philippines. Nearby, the yoga and meditation pavilion is a beautiful spot for a workout, as is the state-of-the-art fitness centre and tennis courts.

Active guests are extremely well catered to at Amanpulo, with all manner of activities from hiking and scuba diving to trying your hand at a jungle obstacle course. Adrenaline junkies should look into the Extreme Water Sports Retreat; a three-night retreat that takes fitness to the next level with everything from beach runs and kite surfing sessions to open water swims and a hike to the summit of Mount English.
