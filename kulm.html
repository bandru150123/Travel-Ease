Having hosted both the 1928 and 1948 Winter Olympic Games, Kulm Hotel St Moritz is no stranger to excellence when it comes to all things snow and ice. As the oldest hotel in a town renowned for its upscale ski resorts, Kulm Hotel has spent over a century and a half perfecting its legacy of luxurious hospitality. The years of experience are evidenced everywhere you look, from impeccably styled interiors to Michelin-starred dining and an enviable location that puts all the best of St. Mortiz – both indoors and outdoors – right on your doorstep.

The 164 rooms and suites lean toward the traditional, though recent renovations have added an element of refreshing, contemporary flair. Stay in the historic main building if you prefer that old-school feel or in the New-Kulm Wing for something a little bit more modern. All rooms – even entry-level – feel bright and airy and have plenty of legroom, though not all have balconies by default. Upgrading to one of the posh Luxury Suites is worth it for the views alone – though the log fires, sophisticated design scheme and liberal use of gorgeous Swiss pine certainly doesn’t hurt.

Five restaurants keep mealtimes interesting no matter how long your stay. The K by Tim Raue highlights the German chef’s Michelin-starred concoctions in a classy – yet casual – dining room. Vegetarians rejoice: the Asian-inspired restaurant has a substantial plant-based menu in addition to its original carnivore-friendly one. The rustic, ski lodge-esque Bob-Restaurant is a lively place for a light lunch between runs on the slopes, and its name is owed to the fact that you’ll have a front-row view of the Bobsled course while you dine. The trattoria-style Pizzeria is a pleasant spot for familiar Italian favourites, while the upscale Grand Restaurant is the place to go if you want an excuse to dress up. For a fabulous Peruvian-meets-a-little-bit-of-everything meal (and an excellent drink), end the day at Sunny Bar by Claudia Canessa (who was named Chef of the Year by Bilanz in 2018).

There’s plenty to do at the Kulm Hotel year-round: ski and ice skate in the winter, play tennis and golf in the summer, and – whatever the season – be sure to visit the heavenly spa where you can unwind and recharge for the days ahead. Don’t skip the jacuzzi while you’re there – the views from it are particularly majestic.

Having hosted both the 1928 and 1948 Winter Olympic Games, Kulm Hotel St Moritz is no stranger to excellence when it comes to all things snow and ice. As the oldest hotel in a town renowned for its upscale ski resorts, Kulm Hotel has spent over a century and a half perfecting its legacy of luxurious hospitality. The years of experience are evidenced everywhere you look, from impeccably styled interiors to Michelin-starred dining and an enviable location that puts all the best of St. Mortiz – both indoors and outdoors – right on your doorstep.

The 164 rooms and suites lean toward the traditional, though recent renovations have added an element of refreshing, contemporary flair. Stay in the historic main building if you prefer that old-school feel or in the New-Kulm Wing for something a little bit more modern. All rooms – even entry-level – feel bright and airy and have plenty of legroom, though not all have balconies by default. Upgrading to one of the posh Luxury Suites is worth it for the views alone – though the log fires, sophisticated design scheme and liberal use of gorgeous Swiss pine certainly doesn’t hurt.

Vegetarians rejoice: the Asian-inspired restaurant has a substantial plant-based menu in addition to its original carnivore-friendly one. The rustic, ski lodge-esque Bob-Restaurant is a lively place for a light lunch between runs on the slopes, and its name is owed to the fact that you’ll have a front-row view of the Bobsled course while you dine. The trattoria-style Pizzeria is a pleasant spot for familiar Italian favourites, while the upscale Grand Restaurant is the place to go if you want an excuse to dress up. For a fabulous Peruvian-meets-a-little-bit-of-everything meal (and an excellent drink), end the day at Sunny Bar by Claudia Canessa (who was named Chef of the Year by Bilanz in 2018).

There’s plenty to do at the Kulm Hotel year-round: ski and ice skate in the winter, play tennis and golf in the summer, and – whatever the season – be sure to visit the heavenly spa where you can unwind and recharge for the days ahead. Don’t skip the jacuzzi while you’re there – the views from it are particularly majestic.
